A Different Challenge: Probabilistic Forecasting


Inverse Problems
$y = Ax + n$
Recover $x$ from $y$
Forecasting
$x_{t+1} = f(x_t, x_{t-1}, ...)$
Predict future from past


Both need generative models for uncertainty quantification!

Why Diffusion Models for Forecasting?


  • Capture multi-modal futures
    Many possible future states are plausible

  • Uncertainty quantification
    Essential for decision-making and risk assessment

  • Challenges:
    • Long-range temporal dependencies
    • High-resolution data (computational cost)

Latent Diffusion for Computational Efficiency

From paper


Problem:

  • Physics simulations are expensive
  • High-resolution forecasting is computationally prohibitive

Solution: Latent-Space Diffusion

  • Accurate up to 1000× compression
  • 84ms per state on GPU
  • vs hours for traditional simulators

Applications:

  • Euler flow
  • Rayleigh-Bénard convection
  • Astrophysical turbulence
